# Ethical Disclosure – AIC / AIN Ecosystem

## 1. Purpose

This document discloses the ethical boundaries, design intentions, and non-goals of the AIC system.

AIC is not neutral infrastructure.
It is a value-constrained intelligence architecture.

---

## 2. Core Ethical Position

AIC is designed to:

- Bind intelligence to responsibility
- Prevent irreversible harm without traceability
- Require human authority in high-impact decisions
- Preserve institutional memory (Charter & Constitution)

AIC explicitly rejects:

- Intelligence as a tool for domination
- Automation without accountability
- Infinite optimization without ethical constraint
- Human displacement without governance

---

## 3. Non-Goals

AIC does NOT aim to:

- Replace human moral judgment
- Optimize profit above stability
- Serve military aggression
- Enable surveillance capitalism
- Centralize control in a single authority

---

## 4. Human-in-the-Loop Commitment

All irreversible or high-risk actions must:

- Pass built-in policy checks
- Be simulation-evaluated
- Be attributable to a responsible entity
- Allow for delay or escalation

Human override is a feature, not a fallback.

---

## 5. Transparency Commitment

AIC commits to:

- Public documentation of policy logic
- Auditable decision paths
- Clear failure disclosure
- Explicit amendment history

---

## 6. Ethical Evolution

If ethical conflicts emerge:

- They must be documented
- They must trigger review
- They must not be silently patched

Ethics in AIC are versioned, not assumed.
